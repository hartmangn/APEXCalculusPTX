Solution 1.1.15.1.

A graph and table of \(f(x) = 1/(x-1)^2\) are given in Figure 1.1.16 and Figure 1.1.17, respectively. Both show that as \(x\) approaches \(1\text{,}\) \(f(x)\) grows larger and larger.

We can deduce this on our own, without the aid of the graph and table. If \(x\) is near 1, then \((x-1)^2\) is very small, and:

\begin{equation*} \frac{1}{\text{ very small number } } = \text{ very large number }\text{.} \end{equation*}

Since \(f(x)\) is not approaching a single number, we conclude that

\begin{equation*} \lim_{x\to 1}\frac{1}{(x-1)^2} \end{equation*}

does not exist.
