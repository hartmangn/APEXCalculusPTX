Item 2.

As \(x\) approaches \(1\) from the right, we consider a limit where all \(x\)-values are greater than \(1\text{.}\) This means we use the \((x-2)^2\) piece of \(f\) as the domain for that piece is \((1,2)\text{.}\) As the \(x\)-values near \(1\text{,}\) \((x-2)^2\) approaches \(1\text{;}\) that is, we see that again \(f(x)\) approaches \(1\text{.}\) Therefore \(\ds \lim_{x\to 1+} f(x)=1\text{.}\)
