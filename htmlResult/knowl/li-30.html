Item 1.

As \(x\) approaches \(1\) from the left, we consider a limit where all \(x\)-values are less than \(1\text{.}\) This means we use the \(2-x\) piece of the piecewise function \(f\) as the domain for that piece is \((0,1)\text{.}\) As the \(x\)-values near \(1\text{,}\) \(2-x\) approaches \(1\text{;}\) that is, \(f(x)\) approaches \(1\text{.}\) Therefore \(\ds \lim_{x\to 1^-} f(x)=1.\)
