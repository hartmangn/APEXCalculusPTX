Item 7.

As \(x\) approaches \(2\) from the left, we consider a limit where all \(x\)-values are less than \(2\text{.}\) This means we use the \((x-2)^2\) piece of \(f\text{.}\) As the \(x\)-values near \(2\text{,}\) \((x-2)^2\) nears \(0\text{;}\) that is, \(f(x)\) approaches \(0\text{.}\) So \(\ds \lim_{x\to 2^-} f(x)=0\text{.}\)
